Naguib Mahfouz 1911 - 2006

“It's a most distressing affliction to have a sentimental heart and a skeptical mind.” ― Naguib Mahfouz, Sugar Street
  • Born in 1911 for a middle class muslim falmiy in Old Cairo.

  • In 1930 he started Studying Philosphy at The Egyptian Universty Then he Decided in 1936 to become a professional Wrtiter before finishing his MA degree.

  • Mahfouz published 34 novels, over 350 short stories, dozens of movie scripts and five plays over a 70-year career.

  • his most famous work, The Cairo Trilogy, depicts the lives of three generations of different families in Cairo from World War I until after the 1952 military coup that overthrew King Farouk.

  • Most of Mahfouz's writings deal mainly with politics, a fact he acknowledged: "In all my writings, you will find politics. You may find a story which ignores love or any other subject, but not politics; it is the very axis of our thinking".

  • Mahfouz was awarded the 1988 Nobel Prize in Literature, the only Arab writer to have won the award.

  • Mahfouz remained a bachelor until age 43 because he believed that, with its numerous restrictions and limitations, marriage would hamper his literary future.
